Research

Themes & Questions

My research is organized around phenomena, not chronology. Each theme represents a sustained line of inquiry into how matter organizes, flows, transitions, and responds at small scales.

Theme 01

Droplet Physics

How tiny volumes organize matter as they vanish.

When a droplet evaporates on a surface, it drives internal flows, concentrates solutes, and deposits structures. These apparently simple systems encode rich physics: capillary flows, Marangoni stresses, contact line dynamics, and vapor-mediated coupling between neighboring drops.

Key questions: What sets the deposition pattern? How does substrate geometry influence the flow field? Can we design evaporation protocols that direct self-assembly?

Methods: Optical microscopy, micro-PIV, interferometry, confocal imaging, controlled atmosphere chambers.

Theme 02

Active Matter, Biology and Beyond

Energy-consuming systems that move, mix, and order themselves.

Active matter describes systems whose constituents consume energy to move living or non living entities, exerting forces on their surroundings. Bacteria, self-propelled colloids, driven suspensions, and synthetic analogs of living matter exhibit collective motion, flocking, and pattern formation that lie outside equilibrium thermodynamics.

Key questions: What sets the transition from disordered to collective motion? How do boundaries and confinement shape active flows? Can active stress be harnessed for transport and assembly?

Methods: Particle tracking, optical microscopy, continuum modeling, dimensional analysis.

Theme 03

Microfluidics, Design & Applications

Chips that route fluids, cells, and signals on demand.

Microfluidic devices enable precise control of fluid environments at scales relevant to cells and molecules. I design and build chip-scale platforms for co-culture experiments, reagent delivery, and real-time biological observation, integrating automation, solenoid valve control, and motorized stages.

Key questions: How can chip topology be optimized for temporal cytokine profiling? What scaling laws govern transport in porous-walled channels? How do we close the loop between sensing and actuation on chip?

Methods: Soft lithography, valve manifold design, Python-based control systems, fluorescence and brightfield microscopy.

Theme 04

Self-Assembly

Order that emerges without a hand to place it.

Liquid-liquid phase separation, colloidal assembly, and DNA nanostructure formation are all examples of order emerging from local rules. I work with synthetic DNA nanostars that form liquid condensates with tunable properties, providing a programmable window into the thermodynamics and kinetics of phase transitions in soft matter and biology.

Key questions: What governs the phase boundaries of DNA liquids? How does valency control material properties? Can synthetic condensates serve as model systems for intracellular organization?

Methods: Fluorescence microscopy, FRAP, rheometry, statistical mechanics modeling.

Theme 05

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Solving the flow before building the device.

My training in mechanical engineering grounds all of my work in heat transfer, fluid mechanics, and computational simulation. Pore-resolved CFD, Brinkman-Forchheimer transport models, and dimensional analysis are design tools, not just analysis tools, for microfluidic and porous systems.

Key questions: What permeability and tortuosity emerge from a given pore geometry? How does flow partition between lumen and wall in porous tubes? What residence time distributions does a chip topology produce?

Methods: OpenFOAM, custom finite-volume solvers, snappyHexMesh, microCT-based geometry reconstruction.

Theme 06

Synergizing AI with the Above

Letting computation see what the eye misses.

Modern experiments generate vast, complex datasets. Computer vision, machine learning, and automated analysis cut across droplet physics, active matter, microfluidics, self-assembly, and CFD, extracting structure that is difficult to see by eye and closing the loop between hypothesis, experiment, and inference.

Applications: Particle tracking in active suspensions, deposition pattern classification in droplets, automated cell detection on chip, segmentation of microCT scans, surrogate models for CFD.

Methods: CLIP, EasyOCR, U-Net segmentation, classical computer vision, scientific Python stack.
