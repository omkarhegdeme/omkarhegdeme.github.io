Ideas & Curiosity

An Open Notebook

This page is a curated collection of questions, speculative threads, and intellectual directions. Not conclusions. Starting points.

Questions I Keep Returning To

Why does the coffee ring exist, and what does it teach us about transport at interfaces?

The coffee ring effect is the most familiar example of evaporation-driven self-assembly. But the conditions under which it breaks down are more interesting than the conditions under which it holds. What controls the transition from ring to uniform to central deposit?

Is there a universal language for how matter organizes itself?

Colloidal crystals, DNA condensates, bacterial colonies, and polymer blends all form structured phases from disordered precursors. The mechanisms differ. But the patterns rhyme. Is there a deeper framework connecting these phenomena?

Can we engineer phase separation?

Biology uses liquid-liquid phase separation to organize the interior of cells. If we can program the phase diagram of synthetic systems, can we build materials that self-organize on demand?

What happens when we stop measuring averages and start watching individuals?

Ensemble measurements mask variability. Single-cell, single-droplet, and single-particle experiments reveal distributions, fluctuations, and rare events that are invisible to bulk measurement. What biology hides in its variance?

Experiments I Want to Build

Automated Droplet Observatory

A platform that deposits hundreds of identical droplets, images each one as it evaporates, and uses computer vision to classify the resulting deposition pattern. The goal: map the full parameter space of evaporation-driven self-assembly.

Vapor Field Imaging

Direct visualization of the vapor concentration field around evaporating droplets using interferometry or schlieren techniques. Currently, vapor-mediated coupling is inferred from deposition asymmetry. I want to see the field itself.

Condensate Microfluidics

Flowing DNA liquid condensates through microfluidic channels to study how shear, confinement, and chemical gradients modify phase behavior. Bridging equilibrium soft matter physics with controlled, out-of-equilibrium engineering.

Living Sensors on Chip

Microfluidic devices where cells act as transducers: responding to chemical perturbations in real time, with fluorescence readout. A platform for studying signaling dynamics at the population level.

Patterns I Find Beautiful

Dendritic crystal growth. The branching of viscous fingers in a Hele-Shaw cell. The ring deposit left by a drying coffee drop. Vortex streets behind cylinders. The fractal edge of a turbulent jet. Spinodal decomposition frozen mid-process. The regular hexagonal cells of Benard convection. The spiraling arms of a bacterial swarm. The phase diagram of water, drawn to scale. The velocity field of a Stokes flow around a sphere. The interference fringes on a thin soap film just before it ruptures.

Notes Toward a Future Lab

A lab that sits at the intersection of fluid physics, microfluidic engineering, biological matter, and computational experiment. A group that builds its own instruments, writes its own analysis code, and publishes work that is simultaneously rigorous and visually compelling. A place where a physicist, a biologist, and an engineer can work on the same experiment. Where the aesthetics of the data are taken as seriously as the statistics. Where every student learns to build before they learn to measure.
