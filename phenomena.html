Mixing in Evaporating Droplets

A single drying drop becomes a miniature reactor.

Capillary flows and Marangoni stresses drive complex recirculation inside sessile droplets. Tracer particles reveal the flow architecture that determines where matter ends up when the solvent is gone.

Particle Trajectories & Micro-PIV

Mapping invisible flow with light and mathematics.

Particle image velocimetry resolves velocity fields at the microscale. Each vector is computed from the correlated motion of thousands of fluorescent tracers, revealing the fluid dynamics that govern transport and deposition.

Crystallization Fronts

Order emerges at the boundary between liquid and solid.

As a saline droplet evaporates, supersaturation triggers nucleation near the contact line. The crystallization front advances inward, its morphology shaped by the competition between diffusion, flow, and confinement.

Vapor-Mediated Droplet Interaction

Drops talk to each other through the air.

When sessile droplets sit close together, their vapor fields overlap. The resulting asymmetry in local evaporation rate drives differential flows and alters deposition, a form of long-range physical communication.

Bacterial Organization in Drying Droplets

Life shapes deposition patterns.

When a droplet containing bacteria dries, the organisms are not merely passive particles. Their motility, interactions, and response to concentration gradients modify the flow and the final deposit structure.

DNA Liquid Condensation

Programmable molecules that phase-separate on command.

DNA nanostars with designed valency undergo liquid-liquid phase separation, forming condensates whose properties, size, viscosity, and composition, can be tuned by sequence and temperature.

Microfluidic Flow Patterns

Laminar precision at the cellular scale.

Microfluidic channels confine fluids to dimensions where viscosity dominates inertia. The resulting laminar flow enables precise control of chemical gradients, mixing, and transport around cells.

CFD Visual Fields

Computation renders the invisible tangible.

Computational fluid dynamics solves the Navier-Stokes equations on discrete grids, producing velocity, pressure, and temperature fields that reveal the structure of flows too complex or too small to measure directly.

Evaporation Deposition Pattern

A fossil record left behind when a droplet dries.

The final deposition pattern of an evaporating droplet records every flow, every gradient, and every nucleation event that occurred during its lifetime. Reading these patterns reveals the physics that produced them.

Co-Culture Chip Architecture

Eighty independently addressable chambers on a glass slide.

A microfluidic chip with 80 chambers arranged in a 5x16 grid, each with valve-controlled reagent delivery, designed for parallel cell co-culture experiments with real-time fluorescence readout.

Fluorescence in Channels

Tracking flow with fluorescent dye.

Fluorescent tracer dye flowing through microfluidic channels, used to validate channel geometry, mixing dynamics, and the laminar profile of the imposed flow.
