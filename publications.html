Papers & Contributions

Selected publications with summaries written for clarity across disciplines. Each paper is placed in context: what question it asks, what it found, and why it matters.

Soft Matter / 2024

Phase Behavior and Material Properties of DNA Nanostar Liquids

This work maps the phase diagram of DNA nanostars with tunable valency, showing how molecular design controls the boundaries and material properties of liquid-liquid phase separation. The results connect programmable DNA chemistry to the physics of biological condensates.

Langmuir / 2022

Vapor-Mediated Interaction and Flow Modification in Neighboring Sessile Droplets

Demonstrates that sessile droplets placed near each other interact through overlapping vapor fields, causing asymmetric evaporation rates that drive modified internal flows and alter final deposition patterns. The effect is measurable at separations of several droplet radii.

droplets vapor coupling evaporation
Paper
Physical Review E / 2021

Crystallization Dynamics in Evaporating Sessile Droplets

Tracks the evolution of crystallization fronts in drying saline droplets, revealing how the competition between diffusive transport and nucleation kinetics selects the final crystal morphology and spatial pattern.

crystallization evaporation pattern formation

Journal of Fluid Mechanics / 2020

Internal Flow and Deposition Patterns in Sessile Droplets with Colloidal Particles

A systematic study of how particle size, concentration, and substrate wettability combine to determine the deposition pattern left behind by an evaporating colloidal droplet. The work links micro-PIV measurements to macroscopic deposit morphology.

Soft Matter / 2019

Bacterial Self-Organization in Evaporating Droplets

Shows that motile bacteria modify the deposition patterns of drying droplets in ways that depend on bacterial concentration, species, and motility. The results have implications for rapid diagnostic assays based on droplet drying patterns.

biofluids droplets bacteria

Lab on a Chip / 2025

Automated Microfluidic Platform for Parallel Co-Culture Experiments

Describes the design and validation of a chip-scale co-culture system with 80 independently addressable chambers, automated reagent delivery via solenoid valve multiplexing, and real-time fluorescence imaging. Built for studying dynamic signaling between cell populations.
